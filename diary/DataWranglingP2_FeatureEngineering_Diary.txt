1. What features you engineered (or transformed), and why?

  1. pct_unhealthy_air (Ratio Transformation):
   
   	How: Calculated by dividing unhealthy_days by days_aqi (total days monitored).

   	Why: Raw counts of unhealthy days are highly misleading. A county with 50 unhealthy days out of 365 monitored is much 	healthier than a county with 50 unhealthy days out of only 100 monitored. This transformation created a standardized metric 	that fairly compares all counties regardless of their EPA monitoring capacity.

  2.income_tier (Categorical Binning): 

 	How: Used quantile binning (pd.qcut) on the median_income column to create four distinct groups: Low, Med-Low, Med-High, and 	High.

	Why: Visualizing the "wealth gap" in environmental justice requires comparing distinct socio-economic brackets. Continuous 	income data is too noisy for clear box plot distributions and dashboard dropdown filters.

  3. pct_over_65 (Ratio Transformation)

	How: Calculated by dividing the senior population (pop_65_and_over) by the total county population (total_pop).

	Why: Raw population counts are heavily biased by county size. Because age is a huge factor for cancer incidence, converting 	this to a percentage isolates the actual demographic age profile of the community.
